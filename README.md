# Synthesis of Copper Oxide (CuO) Nanoparticles by Bottom-Up Approach

## Aim
To synthesize copper oxide (CuO) nanoparticles using a bottom-up approach.

---

## Materials Required

### Chemicals
- Copper nitrate (Cu(NO₃)₂)  
- Sodium hydroxide (NaOH)  
- Distilled water  
- Ethanol (optional for washing)  

### Apparatus Required
- Beaker  
- Conical flask  
- Measuring cylinder  
- Magnetic stirrer with hot plate  
- Burette  
- Thermometer  
- Filter paper  
- pH paper / pH meter  

---

## Formula
w=N*e*l
Where:
- **N** = Normality  
- **e** = Equivalent weight  
- **l** = Volume (in liters)  

---

## Procedure

1. **Preparation of Copper Nitrate Solution**  
   Dissolve copper nitrate (Cu(NO₃)₂) in distilled water and stir well.

2. **Preparation of NaOH Solution**  
   Prepare sodium hydroxide (NaOH) solution separately.

3. **Mixing of Solutions**  
   Add NaOH solution dropwise into the copper nitrate solution under constant stirring.

4. **Formation of Precipitate**  
   A blue precipitate forms initially, which gradually turns black indicating CuO formation.

5. **Heating Process**  
   Maintain the temperature around 60–80°C while stirring continuously.

6. **Filtration and Washing**  
   Filter the precipitate and wash it with distilled water and ethanol to remove impurities.

7. **Drying**  
   Dry the obtained precipitate in a hot air oven.

8. **Calcination**  
   Heat the dried sample at 400–500°C to obtain CuO nanoparticles.
